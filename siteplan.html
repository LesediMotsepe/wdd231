Site Name

Sweet treats

Site Purpose

To delight customers, drive sales, and showcase your brand's personality.

Target Market

Families with kids – looking for fun, kid-friendly desserts.Young adults & millennials (18–35) – drawn to trendy, Instagram-worthy treats.Couples – seeking cozy, shareable dessert experiences.Event planners – in need of custom catering for parties and events.Health-conscious treat lovers – interested in lighter or alternative options. Tourists & locals – exploring unique, memorable dessert spots.

Site Goals

  • Let people see what yummy treats we offer
  • Make it easy to order online or find our location and hours
  • Help more people discover Sweet Treats
  • Show who we are and what makes us special.
  • Let people know about specials, new treats, or parties

User Personas

  • The Sweet Mom: Sarah is a 35-year-old elementary school teacher and a busy mom of two young kids. On weekends, she loves taking her children out for small treats as a reward for good behavior or just to enjoy quality time together. She looks for dessert places that are welcoming, safe, and easy to navigate with kids. When browsing a website, Sarah appreciates clear menu options with pictures, prices, and any allergy information. She also wants to quickly find important details like location, hours, and whether there’s space to sit and relax. If Sweet Treats makes this info easy to find and highlights family-friendly offerings, Sarah is likely to become a repeat visitor.
  • The Event Planner: Jason is a 24-year-old college student with a passion for food and photography. He actively searches for trendy, visually stunning desserts that he can share on his Instagram and TikTok accounts. His decisions are heavily influenced by how "shareable" and "aesthetic" a treat looks. Jason often browses on his phone, so a fast, mobile-friendly site with beautiful photos, clear descriptions, and easy access to social media links is key. He’s drawn to unique flavors, limited-time specials, and fun store designs. If Sweet Treats creates a visually appealing experience online and in-store, Jason will not only become a customer but a promoter.
  • The Instagram Foodie: Amanda is a 42-year-old professional event planner who’s constantly on the hunt for reliable vendors to provide food for weddings, birthdays, and corporate events. She needs to see that Sweet Treats can handle custom orders, bulk requests, and special dietary needs with professionalism. Amanda’s top priorities are reliability, clear communication, and the ability to impress her clients with something unique. When she visits the website, she looks for a catering tab, a gallery of past events, pricing info, and a quick way to contact the team. If Sweet Treats builds trust through well-organized content and a professional tone, Amanda could bring in large, repeat orders.

Scenarios

  • Sarah just finished grocery shopping with her kids on a Saturday morning. She pulls out her phone and searches “dessert shop near me.” She finds Sweet Treats and taps on the website. Right away, she sees cute pictures of colorful cupcakes and a family-friendly vibe. She checks the hours, confirms there’s indoor seating, and looks at the kid-friendly menu options. Seeing a “Family Sundae Special” featured on the homepage, she decides to visit Sweet Treats for a fun treat with her kids.
  • Jason is scrolling through Instagram when he sees a friend post a stunning photo of a milkshake from Sweet Treats. Curious, he clicks the tagged link to the Sweet Treats website. On his phone, he browses the gallery and is impressed by the over-the-top desserts and fun presentation. He checks if there are any limited-time treats on the homepage. Seeing a “Cotton Candy Cloud Shake,” he shares the link with friends and plans a visit so he can film content for his food review account.
  • Amanda is planning a corporate appreciation event and needs a dessert vendor. She searches for "dessert catering near me" and clicks on the Sweet Treats website. She finds a section labeled “Catering & Custom Orders,” which lists package options and shows photos from previous events. She fills out the quick inquiry form to request a quote and sees testimonials from past customers that give her confidence. A few hours later, she gets a friendly follow-up email and moves forward with a bulk order.
